Reflection

Looking back
on my four years.

To be completely honest, the goal of my career is not to find some super high-paying job to become rich. Instead of having to go through a work-intensive career, I would like to find a calm job that pays a moderate salary and live comfortably off of that. I would be happy with lower pay computer science jobs, as long as I am happy working there, the money doesn’t matter that much

FRESHMAN YEAR — The Beginning

Finding My Direction

I arrived at Mizzou as an undecided engineering student unsure of what I wanted to do. After taking my first computer science course, I felt that I really resonated with the type of work that is done in this field. This is when I decided I would major in computer science.

SOPHOMORE & JUNIOR YEARS — Building Skills

Learning to Build Real Things

Taking Computer Science courses, I was able to gain experience in databases, algorithms, and software engineering. Inside and outside of classes. I used these skills to build projects that I am proud of. Websites like Tiger Trades, Terraplot, and more, I kept building on my skills until I reached a point where I had a solid portfolio of projects.

SENIOR YEAR — Now

Machine Learning & the Final Stretch

In my senior year, I decided to explore machine learning. Building an ensemble ML model for loan prediction with SMOTE-Tomek resampling, threshold optimization, and explainability tooling showed me how deep computer science can go beyond writing basic code. Completing my loan prediction app rounded out a portfolio I am genuinely proud of.

SPRING 2026 — Looking Ahead

What Comes Next

Graduating with a CS degree, a Mathematics minor, and an IT minor feels like a big accomplishment for me. I have been on the search for a job that would allow me to express the skills I have built up over these four years at Mizzou. I am hopeful that I will find a good fit and feel good about the work I put out each day.
